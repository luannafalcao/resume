Seven Knowledges

Visual Identity for UNESCO Conference

Visual Identity – UNESCO Conference

Context

In 2010, I was invited to design the visual identity for the International Conference "The Seven Knowledges Necessary for the Education of the Present", held in Fortaleza, Brazil. The event marked the International Day of Peace and was endorsed by UNESCO, drawing inspiration from the principles of French philosopher Edgar Morin.

Challenge

The main challenge was to visually represent the concept of “seven knowledges” — principles that transcend disciplinary boundaries and advocate for a holistic, ethical, and planetary-conscious approach to education. The design needed to communicate clarity, symbolism, and institutional credibility to an international audience of educators and policymakers.

Design Solution

The final logo depicts the planet Earth embraced by seven colorful arcs, each symbolizing one of the seven interconnected dimensions of knowledge. The vibrant palette reflects diversity and movement, while the clean typography balances accessibility with formal presence — appropriate for both printed and digital institutional use.

Recognition

The logo was officially approved by UNESCO and featured in all conference materials — including banners, printed programs, and digital publications such as the Carta de Fortaleza, a document of educational recommendations that circulated internationally among academic and governmental networks.

Personal Impact

This project marked a turning point in my early design career, reinforcing my passion for creating visuals that support cultural, educational, and socially impactful initiatives. These values continue to shape both my UX work and independent art projects to this day.
